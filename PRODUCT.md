# PRODUCT.md — kerryhatcher.com

A personal site and writing space at www.kerryhatcher.com. Built with Jekyll, run through podman, deployed as a static site.

## Register

**brand** — the design IS the product. A reader's first impression of the page is the deliverable. No transactional UI, no app surface.

## Users

One real audience: people who landed here from a link in a post, a talk, or a referral. They came to read something specific or to figure out who Kerry is. They do not want a tour, a hero pitch, or a CTA. They want to read.

A secondary audience: Kerry, who will write here often enough that the act of opening a draft and publishing needs to feel inviting rather than ceremonial.

## Voice

Three concrete words: **deliberate, warm, opinionated.** Not "modern" or "elegant." Closer to: a well-printed indie zine that argues for its point of view; a letter you actually wanted to receive; a workshop with the door open.

## Anti-references

What this site is explicitly **not**:

- Not a SaaS landing page. No hero metric, no feature grid, no CTA-above-the-fold.
- Not a magazine clone. No drop caps, no italic Fraunces display, no broadsheet grid as scaffolding.
- Not a developer-portfolio dark mode. No green-on-black terminal aesthetic for its own sake.
- Not the default Jekyll theme aesthetic (Minima, Chirpy, Hydejack). Those are templates; this is identity.

## Strategic principles

- **Reading wins.** Long-form posts get the best typography, the widest margins, the calmest treatment. Everything else defers to that surface.
- **One bold color.** A single committed accent carries the brand. Hedging with three brand colors makes it look like a startup.
- **Animation is choreography, not garnish.** Each motion has a reason: a reveal that times to scroll arrival, a progress indicator that maps to reading, an underline that signals interactivity.
- **Light register is the default.** People read better on light backgrounds in the contexts this site is opened in. Dark mode is a courtesy, not the headliner.
